Chef Vin de Jean-Taex was raised in Tibet by a group of hedonistic monks who all shared one thing in common: the love of fine food. Unfortunately, living on a mountain and freezing your ass off does not always provide the mossiest creative environment; the journey from braised sheepshead over an open flame to the finest kitchen in Atlanta was both arduous and circuitous.

In 1997, the young genius backpacked his way to Eastern Europe eventually making his way to Prague. At a chance encounter in a dank opium den, Jean-Taex met the famed restauranteur, Claude Tiersmacht. A heated debate ensued over the proper way to prepare sweet-meats, but the the two immediately connected and a grand partnership was born.

On a ski weekend in Gstaad, Tiersmacht invited the young chef to partner with him on his newest venture, the famed Subway franchise in Akron on 18th Street. Jean-Tex consented and the people of northern Ohio are forever thankful.

In 2012, Blowfish opened in downtown Atlanta to critical acclaim. The AJC’s head restaurant critic, John Kessler proclaimed, ‘Never before have I eaten such fine food. The world owes Blowfish a debt of gratitude’. This prompted a visit by the Times’ Pete Wells to visit and announce, ‘I’m moving. New York simply doesn’t offer anything of this caliber. Brava!!!’.

At Blowfish, you will experience what critics and plebeians alike have noticed. Simply put, Jean-Taex is a master at maintaining the essence of ingredients while using them inventively to produce the best flavor. Tiersmacht’s chic and intimate dining room, designed by Thomas Juul‑Hansen, will be the finishing touch on a memorable evening, either spent with your lover, or your grandma … or both! Come see, taste, and experience what is … Blowfish.
